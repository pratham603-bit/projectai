Renaissance

The Renaissance was a cultural movement that revived interest in classical art, literature, and learning. Notable figures include Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Raphael. This period also saw the beginning of modern science with pioneers like Copernicus and Galileo challenging old beliefs.

Innovation

A significant shift occurred toward human potential and achievements, influencing education, philosophy, and politics. Innovations during this time included the mechanical clock, which revolutionized timekeeping; eyeglasses, which improved vision for scholars and craftsmen; anvils, essential for blacksmiths; and gunpowder, introduced from China, which transformed warfare by making firearms and cannons possible.

Beginning of the Mughal Empire

The Mughal Empire was established by Babur in 1526 after defeating Ibrahim Lodi at the Battle of Panipat. Akbar the Great is known for his policies of religious tolerance, administrative reforms, and cultural achievements.

The Mughal Empire is also famous for its architectural marvels like the Taj Mahal, Red Fort, and Fatehpur Sikri, which reflect the grandeur and sophistication of Mughal architecture.
